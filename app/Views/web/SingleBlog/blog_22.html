Courses Offered for MBBS Aspirants


  • USU's MBBS program is a six-year, full-time course designed to equip students with the knowledge, skills, and values required to excel in the medical field.
  • The program is divided into two phases: pre-clinical and clinical.
    • During the preclinical phase, students delve into the theoretical foundations of medicine, covering subjects like anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, and pharmacology.
    • The clinical phase, on the other hand, provides practical training through hospital rotations, allowing students to hone their clinical skills under the guidance of experienced doctors.

Syllabus: A Holistic Approach to Medical Education


USU's MBBS program adheres to the highest educational standards, ensuring students receive a comprehensive and well-rounded education. The syllabus is designed to balance theoretical knowledge with practical experience, incorporating the latest advancements in medical science and technology. Here's a glimpse into some of the key subjects covered in the program:


  • Pre-clinical Phase:

    • Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry, Histology, Microbiology, Pharmacology, Pathology


  • Clinical Phase:

    • Internal Medicine, Surgery, Pediatrics, Obstetrics and Gynecology, Psychiatry, Public Health.
