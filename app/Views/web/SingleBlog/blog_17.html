About Andijan State Medical Institute


Andijan State Medical Institute (ASMI) is a public medical university located in Andijan, Uzbekistan. It was established in 1991 and offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs in medicine, pharmacy, and nursing. The university has a faculty of over 1,000 and more than 5,000 students. ASMI is recognized by the World Health Organization and has partnerships with universities in the United States, Europe, and Asia. The university has a modern campus with state-of-the-art facilities and equipment. Moreover, Andijan State Medical Institute has ranked among the top medical institutes and research centers that offers outstanding educational benefits along with the best services for studying MBBS in Uzbekistan.

About Andijan City


Andijan, the largest city in the Fergana Valley with a population of 350,000, stands as Uzbekistan's foremost center for both engineering and culture. Positioned in the southeastern reaches of the valley, it epitomizes the nation's Uzbek essence, both linguistically and culturally. Serving as the capital of the Andijan region, the city experiences a continental climate, marked by stark differentials between winter and summer temperatures, with an average annual temperature of 13.7°C.
